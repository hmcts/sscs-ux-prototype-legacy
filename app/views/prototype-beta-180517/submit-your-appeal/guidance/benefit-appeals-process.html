Benefit appeals process


Appeal received

We lodge your appeal and send a copy to the department which made the decision about your entitlement to benefits. This will either be DWP, HMRC or your local authority.

Appeal response

The department which made the decision about your entitlement to benefits writes a response. This should explain how they’ve made their decision and include any evidence

Hearing booked

We book a hearing for your appeal. It’s an informal opportunity for you to explain your reasons for appealing. We can make arrangements so the hearing can cater for your needs.

Hearing

An informal hearing takes place in front of a panel of people called a tribunal. They are independent and will make an impartial decision.

How long does an appeal take?

It depends on a lot of factors, such as the number of appeals we receive and how busy your local hearing venue is. You can avoid delays to your appeal by:

  • sending any evidence well before the hearing (if you have any)
  • turning up to the hearing on time
  • getting a representative early (if you need one)
