2. Benefit appeals process

Your appeal will go through the following steps after you’ve submitted it.


Appeal received

We record your appeal and send a copy to the department (DWP or HMRC) which made the decision.

Response

The department provide a written response which explains how they made their decision.

Hearing booked

We book a hearing for your appeal. It’s an opportunity for you to explain your appeal. It’s up to you whether you come.

Hearing

An informal hearing takes place at a tribunal venue. The tribunal will decide whether the decision was correct.

How long does this take?

Unfortunately we can’t say how long your appeal will take. It depends on too many factors. We can say that it will be at least X weeks but it could be much longer.

To avoid delays to your appeal you should do the following:

  • send any evidence well before the hearing
  • make yourself available for a hearing any time
  • turn up to the hearing on time
