Overview

You can use this service to appeal a decision that was made about your entitlement to benefits. For example Personal Independence Payment (PIP), Employment and Support Allowance (ESA) or Housing Benefit (HB).

The tribunal is independent and will decide whether the decision about your entitlement to benefits was correct.

Before you appeal

For most benefits you have to ask the DWP to reconsider their decision before you appeal. This is known as requesting mandatory reconsideration.

If you’re appealing a decision about Housing Benefit then you should contact your local authority before appealing. For Child Benefit you should phone HMRC first.

Find out more about requesting mandatory reconsideration.

You should appeal within one month of receiving the MRN. You can still appeal if it’s over that time but you’ll need to explain why it’s late.
