Benefit appeals process


Appeal received

We record your appeal and send a copy to the department which made the decision about your entitlement to benefits. This will either be the DWP, HMRC or your local authority.

Response

The department which made the decision about your entitlement to benefits provides a written response to your appeal.

Hearing booked

We book a hearing for your appeal. It’s an opportunity for you to explain your reasons for appealing. It’s up to you whether you come.

Hearing

An informal hearing takes place. The tribunal is independent and will decide whether the decision was correct.

How long does this take?

Unfortunately we can’t say how long it will take. It depends on too many factors.

To avoid delays to your appeal you should:

  • send any evidence well before the hearing
  • make yourself available for a hearing any time
  • turn up to the hearing on time
  • get a representative early (if you need one)
