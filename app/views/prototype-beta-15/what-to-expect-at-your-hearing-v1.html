What to expect at your hearing

Your hearing is where you explain your case and get a decision on your benefit appeal. The tribunal is independent from DWP and will listen to both sides of the dispute before making a decision.

When you arrive

You’ll be greeted by a tribunal clerk. They’ll take care of you at the hearing venue and answer any questions you may have. You’ll sit in a waiting area until your hearing begins.

The hearing room

The tribunal clerk will take you into a room for your hearing. It’s more like a meeting room than a courtroom. The judge and other panel members will be sat behind a table. You’ll be invited to sit opposite them. If you have someone to support you or to give evidence, they’ll sit next to you.

A typical benefit appeal hearing with a judge, doctor and disability expert. Please note that sometimes hearings may take place in a courtroom.

The people at your hearing

The tribunal is made up of a panel of people who’ll make a balanced decision on your appeal.

The judge runs the hearing. They’ll explain how it works. They’ll also take notes throughout. This is to create a record of the hearing.

A doctor and a disability expert sit with the judge in Personal Independence Payment (PIP) appeals. They provide medical and disability advice. In Employment Support Allowance (ESA) appeals there’ll just be a doctor with the judge.

DWP

A representative from DWP may be there. They are not on the panel and are there to speak on behalf of DWP. This representative won’t be the person who made the decision about your benefits.

During your hearing

The judge and the other panel members will ask you some straightforward questions. They’re trying to understand how your condition affects your life. They may ask how you cope with certain tasks or about the types of of things you need help with. This takes about an hour.

Sometimes the panel will need more evidence or information to help them make a decision. It’s important that you submit all your evidence well before the hearing so they have time to review it. Otherwise they may have to postpone the hearing to a different day.

Getting a decision

After you’ve answered all the questions you’ll be asked to wait outside while the panel members discuss your appeal. You’ll then be asked back into the room to be told the decision.

The judge will explain whether you’re entitled to the benefit and if so, at what level.

Sometimes the tribunal may need more time to make a decision. If that happens the decision about your appeal will be posted to you.
