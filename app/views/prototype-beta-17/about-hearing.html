What to expect at your hearing

Your hearing is an opportunity for you to explain your appeal and get an impartial decision on your entitlement to Employment and Support Allowance (ESA). The tribunal is independent and will consider both sides of the appeal.

An ESA appeal hearing with a judge and a medical expert.

When you arrive

You’ll be greeted by a tribunal clerk. They’ll take care of you at the hearing venue and answer any questions you have.

The hearing room

The tribunal clerk will take you into the hearing room. The judge and medical expert will be sitting at a table. You’ll be invited to sit opposite them. If you have someone to support you or to give evidence, they’ll sit next to you.

The people at your hearing

The tribunal is made up of a judge and a medical expert who will make an impartial decision on your appeal. They are both independent from DWP.

The judge runs the hearing and will explain how it works. They’ll also take notes of what is said during the hearing.

The medical expert is there to give their medical opinion on your appeal.

DWP

A representative from DWP might be there. They are not on the panel and are there to speak on behalf of DWP. They won’t be the person who made the decision about your entitlement to ESA.

During your hearing

The judge and medical expert will ask you some straightforward questions. They’re trying to understand how your condition affects your life. It’s your opportunity to explain your side of the appeal. The whole hearing takes up to an hour.

Sometimes the they will need more evidence to help them make a decision. It’s important that you submit all your evidence well before the hearing so they have time to review it. Otherwise they may have to postpone the hearing to a different day.

Getting a decision

After you’ve answered all the questions you’ll be asked to wait outside while the tribunal discusses your appeal. You’ll then be asked back into the room to be told the decision.

The judge will explain whether you’re entitled to ESA and if so, at what level.

Sometimes the tribunal may need more time to make a decision. If that happens the decision about your appeal will be posted to you.
