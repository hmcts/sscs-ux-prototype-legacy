3. Help with your appeal

There’s various help and support you can get for your appeal, from representatives who can advise you to telephone support from the tribunal.

Representatives

If you think you’ll be unable to manage your appeal on your own you may want the help of a representative. They can help you with some or all of the following:

  • Advise you on the law in relation to your appeal
  • Help you prepare evidence to support your appeal
  • Help you prepare a written statement for the tribunal
  • Talk to us about your appeal on your behalf
  • Help you explain your appeal at the hearing
  • Advise you on what to do after the tribunal has made their decision

You can assign anyone to be your representative, including friends and family.

Anyone who is registered as a representative will be sent confidential information about you, such as medical evidence. They can also make decisions on your appeal but should only do this with your permission.

Telephone support

If you have a question about the appeal process, you can contact the HMCTS Contact Centre. They can’t provide you with legal advice.

Telephone: 0300 123 1142
Monday to Friday, 8:30am to 5pm

Financial support

The appeal can take several months. If you think you’ll struggle for money you should contact DWP, Jobcentre or your local council to see whether they can help.
