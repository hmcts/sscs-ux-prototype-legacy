Before you appeal

For most benefits you have to ask DWP to reconsider their decision before you appeal. This is known as requesting 'mandatory reconsideration'. DWP will then send you a Mandatory Reconsideration Notice (MRN) which tells you whether they have changed their decision.

If you’re appealing a decision about Housing Benefit then you should phone your local authority before appealing. For Child Benefit you should phone HMRC first.

You can find their contact details on any letter you’ve received about the benefit.

You should appeal within a month after going through 'mandatory reconsideration'. You might still be able to appeal after one month but you’ll need to explain why it’s late.
