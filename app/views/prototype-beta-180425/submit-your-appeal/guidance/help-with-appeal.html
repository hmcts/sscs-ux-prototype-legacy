3. Help with your appeal

There’s various support available before and during your appeal.

Representatives

If you think you’ll be unable to manage your appeal on your own you may want the help of a representative. They often work for a charity or welfare rights organisation and will provide their services for free.

A good representative can help you do one or more of the following:

  • Advise you on the appeals process
  • Help you prepare evidence to support your appeal
  • Help you prepare a written statement
  • Act on your behalf during your appeal
  • Help you explain your appeal at the hearing
  • Advise you on what to do after the tribunal have made a decision

Anyone can be your representative, including friends and family.

Anyone who is registered as a representative will be sent all the information relating to your appeal, including medical evidence. They can also make decisions on your appeal but should only do this with your permission.

Telephone support

Telephone support is available if you need help using the service. Contact a representative if you need legal advice (see section above).

Financial support

Your appeal can take several months. If you think you’ll struggle for money you should contact DWP, Jobcentre Plus or your local council to see whether they can help.
