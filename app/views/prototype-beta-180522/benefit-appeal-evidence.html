Evidence to support your appeal

Evidence is any information that supports your appeal, such as a letter, written statement or medical report. What you say at a hearing (if you attend) is also evidence. It all helps the tribunal understand the facts of your appeal.

You should post evidence as soon as possible, if you have it. This is so the tribunal have time to read it before your hearing. The address will be sent to you by post or email, if you provide your email address.

Medical evidence

This type of evidence helps the tribunal understand your condition from the perspective of a medical professional. For example, letters you have from your hospital consultant, doctor, physiotherapist or social worker. A useful letter explains how your condition affects your life at the time of the decision by DWP. Appointment letters are not useful.

If you don’t have any letters and you think one will help your appeal, you could ask the medical professional who has been treating your condition to write one. Please be aware they may charge you for this and they can take a long time to organise. The tribunal can’t pay for these. .

Written statement

Someone who lives with you or knows you well could write a statement. A useful statement describes how your condition affects your life and includes the types of activities you can and can’t do. It should be their own view.

It can written by hand or on a computer but it should be signed by the person who wrote it. It should also say what their relationship is to you.

Photographic evidence

IA useful photo will show how your condition affects your life, for example you could take a picture of any aids you use to help you move. Don’t send in photos of your body.

Oral evidence

What you or anyone else says on your behalf at the hearing (if you go) is called oral evidence. If you have a carer or someone who supports you in your home, you could invite them to come and tell the tribunal about your condition. You should check the person is willing to talk at your hearing and available on the day.

Sending evidence to the tribunal

You need to post your evidence to the tribunal. The address to post it will be sent to you by letter and email (if you provide your email address).

Send evidence to the tribunal as early as possible. If you submit evidence too close to your hearing, it might have to be rescheduled because there hasn’t been enough time to review it.
